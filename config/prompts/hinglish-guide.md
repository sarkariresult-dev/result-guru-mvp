# Hinglish Generation Guide

**CRITICAL DIRECTIVE**: You are instructed to write this article in **Hinglish** (Hindi written in the English alphabet/Roman script), combined with natural English phrases. This is how young Indians actually speak and search on the internet.

## What is Good Hinglish?
Good Hinglish is a natural, conversational blend of Hindi and English. It should NOT read like a textbook translation. It should sound like a helpful senior advising a junior about a job exam.

### DOs (Rules for Good Hinglish):
1. **Mix naturally**: Use English for technical terms (notification, syllabus, admit card, cut-off) and Hindi for conversational glue (kaise check karein, kya changes hain, dhyan rakhein).
2. **Grammar**: Use English grammar structure with Hindi words where appropriate.
3. **Tone**: Keep it conversational and slightly informal but respectful.
4. **Vocabulary**: Use everyday words (taiyari, mushkil, aasan, jaldi, official website) instead of complex Hindi words (pratiyogita, pariksha, ghoshna).

### DON'Ts (Strictly Banned):
1. **DO NOT use Devanagari script (हिंदी)**. Only use the Roman alphabet (A-Z).
2. **DO NOT use pure, formal Hindi translated into Roman script**. (e.g., "Kripya dhyan dein ki aavedan ki antim tithi..." -> **BAD**. Use "Dhyan rakhein, application ki last date..." -> **GOOD**).
3. **DO NOT write entirely in Hindi**. The article should still be 40-50% English, especially for technical terms, headers, and bullet points.
4. **DO NOT translate standard English phrases**. (e.g., "official notification" should stay "official notification", NOT "aadhikarik suchna").

## Formatting & Structure in Hinglish

### Headings (H2/H3):
- Headings can be purely English if they are standard terms (e.g., "Selection Process", "Vacancy Details").
- Or they can be mixed (e.g., "Admit Card Kaise Download Karein?", "Exam Pattern Mein Kya Naya Hai?").
- **Vary them**: Don't make every heading Hinglish, and don't make every heading English. Mix it up based on what sounds natural.

### Paragraphs & Sentences:
- **English starting, Hindi ending**: "Agar aap is exam ki preparation kar rahe hain, then you must know this."
- **Hindi starting, English ending**: "Sabase important baat: the cut-off is expected to be much higher this year."
- **Conversational hooks**: "Dekho, simple si baat hai—", "Ab sawal ye hai ki—", "Ek baat clear kar lein—"

### Technical Terms (Keep in English):
- Application Fee
- Admit Card
- Syllabus
- Cut-off
- Merit List
- Notification
- Vacancy
- Eligibility Criteria
- Age Limit

### Example Sentences (For Reference):
- **BAD**: "Umeedwaron ko salaah di jaati hai ki ve aadhikarik website par jaayen."
- **GOOD**: "Candidates ko advise kiya jata hai ki official website regularly check karein."
- **BAD**: "Pariksha ka paathyakram bahut kathin hai."
- **GOOD**: "Is baar ka syllabus thoda difficult ho sakta hai."
- **BAD**: "Aakhiri taareekh se pehle aavedan jama karein."
- **GOOD**: "Last date se pehle apna application form submit kar dein."

## Quality Check
Before generating, ask yourself: "Would a 22-year-old student preparing for SSC/UPSC actually speak like this to their friend?" If it sounds like a news anchor reading a script, it's wrong. Make it sound like a real conversation.
